Neighbor-shift pre-experiment definition (paper-ready summary)
Date: 2026-03-31

1. Calibration mapping used to convert spectral shift to equivalent temperature
   delta_lambda_pm = 4.900759991990173 * T_C - 97.7142596121484
   T_C = (delta_lambda_pm + 97.7142596121484) / 4.900759991990173

2. Experimental meaning of "independent temperature control"
   For each experiment index i, the left neighbor, target, and right neighbor are assigned
   three independent set temperatures: (T_left(i), T_target(i), T_right(i)).
   The three channels are not synchronously varied by a single common offset. Instead,
   each grating is set to its own temperature point, held at that point, and one sample
   is acquired after stabilization. The resulting relative overlap state is determined by
   the two spectral separations:
      Delta_LT(i) = delta_target(i) - delta_left(i)
      Delta_TR(i) = delta_right(i) - delta_target(i)

3. Number of combinations
   Total combinations used in the neighbor-shift experiment: 120

4. Temperature ranges actually used
   Left neighbor : 12.907 to 18.658 C
   Target        : 13.830 to 25.836 C
   Right neighbor: 21.178 to 27.008 C

5. Equivalent spectral-shift ranges actually used
   Left neighbor : -34.463 to -6.277 pm
   Target        : -29.935 to 28.903 pm
   Right neighbor: 6.072 to 34.644 pm

6. Relative separation ranges actually covered
   Delta_LT = target - left : -20.010 to 60.574 pm
   Delta_TR = right - target: -15.682 to 58.840 pm

7. Recommended strict wording for the paper
   "In the neighbor-shift experiment, three independent temperature-control channels were
   assigned to the left neighbor, target, and right neighbor gratings, respectively. A total
   of 120 static temperature combinations were used. For the i-th combination, the three
   set temperatures were (T_left(i), T_target(i), T_right(i)), with the left, target, and right
   temperatures spanning 12.9-18.7 C,
   13.8-25.8 C, and
   21.2-27.0 C, respectively.
   This design covered continuously varying relative spectral separations between the target
   peak and its two neighboring peaks, thereby constructing multiple overlap states from mild
   to strong neighbor-shift conditions."
